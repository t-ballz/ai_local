## Summary
This paper systematically evaluates agent-native memory architectures from a data management perspective, decomposing memory systems into four modules: representation/storage, extraction, retrieval/routing, and maintenance. Through comprehensive analysis, it shows that no single architecture excels universally—effectiveness depends on workload alignment. Key findings: graph-based systems excel at temporal reasoning; retrieval benefits from structured evidence; conservative consolidation outperforms aggressive summarization.
