# From AGI to ASI

> Source: [arXiv:2606.12683](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12683) · Google DeepMind · June 12, 2026  
> Authors: Tim Genewein, Shane Legg, Thore Graepel, Allan Dafoe, Joel Z. Leibo, et al.

## TL;DR

A Google DeepMind report on the trajectory from AGI to ASI. Not a prediction of timelines — it explicitly avoids that — but a framework for *how* the transition might happen, what bottlenecks could slow it, and why measuring and forecasting AI progress is itself a major research problem. The core argument: ASI arrives not as a single event but as a series of compounding societal transformations.

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition used in the paper |
|------|------------------------------|
| **AGI** | A system achieving median human-level performance across most cognitive tasks — roughly one competent human |
| **ASI** | A system exceeding large expert collectives across virtually all tasks and domains |
| **UAI** | Theoretical upper bound: AIXI — optimal performance over all computable tasks (not achievable in practice) |

The gap between AGI and ASI is the subject of this report. The paper argues this gap is not a step but a long traversal, and the route matters.

---

## Four pathways from AGI to ASI

| Pathway | Mechanism | Dynamic |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Scale** | Continued growth in compute, data, model size | ~10× effective compute/year (hardware × investment × algorithmic gains) |
| **Paradigm shift** | New architecture or optimisation breakthrough beyond transformers | Discontinuous; hard to forecast |
| **Recursive self-improvement** | AI accelerating AI R&D | Potentially super-exponential; high uncertainty |
| **Multi-agent coordination** | Emergent superintelligence from many AGI agents working together | Depends heavily on coordination quality |

The ~10× per year compute estimate comes from: ~1.5× hardware gains × ~2.5× investment growth × ~3× algorithmic efficiency improvements — all of which have been sustained in recent history.

---

## Five bottlenecks that could slow the transition

1. **Data wall** — High-quality text is finite; synthetic data and simulation are partial mitigations
2. **Economic limits** — Exponential investment growth cannot continue indefinitely
3. **Energy and infrastructure** — Physical constraints on power and chip manufacturing
4. **Research diminishing returns** — Capability improvements may require exponentially more input effort
5. **Abstraction barrier** — Whether current paradigms can achieve the abstract reasoning needed for general intelligence remains unresolved

The report is careful to note these are *bottlenecks*, not ceilings. Each could be circumvented by one of the four pathways above.

## Digital advantages of AI systems

The paper lists structural properties AI systems have over biological intelligence:

- **Substrate independence** — can run on any hardware, not tied to biology
- **Parallelisable execution** — many copies running simultaneously
- **Perfect memory copying** — model weights transferred exactly, unlike imperfect human knowledge transfer
- **Flexible timescale** — can run faster or slower than biological cognition

These advantages compound: a system that is marginally AGI-level gains access to digital amplification that humans do not.

---

## What the paper is NOT

- A prediction of AGI or ASI arrival dates (explicitly disclaimed)
- A safety paper (though authors include safety researchers; safety is mentioned but not the focus)
- A model benchmark paper

It is a **forecasting framework** — a structured way of thinking about the variables, their interactions, and what we would need to measure to improve our predictions.

---

## The research agenda the paper calls for

The authors conclude that **measuring and forecasting AI progress is itself a major research field** — currently underinvested. Specifically needed:

- Quantitative models of the four pathways with regular updates as evidence accumulates
- Understanding of recursive self-improvement dynamics and whether they lead to super-exponential growth
- Multi-agent coordination efficiency measurements
- Bottleneck severity tracking over time
